History of Bossa Nova Music

Early History

Bossa Nova began on the tropical beaches of Rio de Janeiro in the late 1950s. The samba-based sound was created by a small group of middle-class students, artists, and musicians. The music was a celebration of youth culture and romance.

    Early Players
  • Antonio Carlos Jobim
  • Joao Gilberto
  • Vincinius de Moraes

Global Connections

Bossa Nova reached global popularity after being discovered by American A&R professionals and recorded professionally in American studios. Jazz musicians in the US began trying to emulate the sound at home soonafter

The genre reached peak popularity in the early 60s after American Stan Getz performed "The Girl from Ipanema" with Joao and Astrud Gilberto at New York's Carnegie Hall. The song spent 96 weeks on US charts and is currently the second most played song of all time behind the Beatles' Yesterday
